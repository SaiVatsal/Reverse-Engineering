

In Challenge 4 of Dev Weekend 47, you step into the role of a lead reverse engineer tasked with salvaging a corrupted file. The hacker attempted to erase evidence by mutilating an archive named corrupted_archive.bin, but investigators managed to seize it before it was completely lost. The operating system cannot recognize the file because its magic bytes—the signature that identifies it as a ZIP archive—were deliberately removed. Your mission is to reconstruct the archive by restoring the missing header bytes (50 4B 03 04, which correspond to PK\x03\x04 in ASCII), saving the repaired data as a valid .zip file, and extracting the hidden flag.txt inside.

The process involves four main steps: reading the corrupted binary data into memory, prepending the correct magic bytes, writing the patched sequence into a new file called repaired_archive.zip, and finally using Python’s zipfile module to open the archive and recover the flag. Once completed, the script demonstrates how forensic techniques can bypass deliberate corruption and recover sensitive evidence. The expected output confirms success by printing the restored flag to the terminal, proving that even when attackers try to destroy data, careful reverse engineering can bring it back.
